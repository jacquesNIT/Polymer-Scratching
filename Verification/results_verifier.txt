Results Verification Criteria


A. Numerical Quality

1. Quasi-static Control (KE/IE)

    Description: Ensures that inertial forces remain negligible compared to internal deformation forces.

    Ratio = ALLKE(t) / ALLIE(t) x 100% < 5%

2. Hourglass Energy (AE/IE)

    Description: Controls the artificial energy introduced by restoring forces to counter the zero-energy deformation modes of C3D8R elements.

    Ratio = ALLAE(t_final) / ALLIE(t_final) x 100% < 10%

3. Total Energy Conservation (ETOTAL)

    Description: Detects numerical instabilities (distorted elements, irregular contact).

    Drift = |ETOTAL(t_f) - ETOTAL(t_i)| / max(|ETOTAL|, |ALLIE|) x 100% < 1%


B. Material Consistency (Mooney-Rivlin)

4. D1 Validity (K/mu Window)

    Description: Prevents artificial compressibility if the ratio is too low,
                 or single-precision numerical noise accompanied by a drop in the time increment if D1 is too small.

    Criterion: K/mu in [10, 100]

    Derived formulas: mu_0 = 2(C10 + C01)
                      K_0  = 2/D1
                      E_0  = 9*K*mu / (3K + mu)
                      nu_0 = (3K - 2mu) / (2(3K + mu))

5. Force Magnitude (Hertz Estimate)

    Description: Order-of-magnitude verification of the maximum normal force RF2 against the Hertz analytical model
                 to detect potential unit or calibration errors.

    Formulas: F_Hertz = (4/3) * E* * sqrt(R) * delta^(3/2)
              E*      = E_0 / (1 - nu_0^2)

              Implied depth: delta_implied = (RF2_peak / ((4/3) * E* * sqrt(R)))^(2/3) < R

              A x10 tolerance is applied.

    Based on the assumptions of small strain, frictionless, normal contact.

6. Strain Level and Strain Rate

    Description: Ensures the characteristic strain remains within the validity range of the Mooney-Rivlin model.

    Formulas (Tabor): a         = sqrt(delta * R)             [contact radius]
                      eps_char  = 0.2 * a / R < 1.0          [100% strain limit]
                      v         = scratch_length / scratch_time
                      t_contact = 2a / v
                      eps_dot   = eps_char / t_contact


C. Physical Consistency

7. Friction Physics (SCOF)

    Description: Validates the consistency of the apparent friction coefficient, which combines
                 the input adhesive friction and the ploughing resistance.

    Formulas & Conditions: SCOF = |RF3| / |RF2|

        (a) SCOF >= mu_input
        (b) SCOF <= mu_input + 0.5    (with mu_p ~ (2/pi)(a/R))

8. Full Recovery

    Description: Since the Mooney-Rivlin model is hyperelastic and dissipation-free, the material must
                 fully return to its initial state (zero residual depth) after unloading.

    Residual depth = |min(y_deformed, 0)|
    Relative       = residual_depth / scratch_depth x 100% < 5%


Results and remarks


1. Quasi-static Control (KE/IE)

    The kinetic energy is very low for most simulations, especially with longer scratching times (0.02s -> 50%, 2s -> 0.03%). (~v**2)
    This means it should not be an issue when testing realistic scratching times (200s), which is in work.
    For all simulations, it converges nicely towards 0 with mass scaling and is independent of mesh size. (~m)

2. Hourglass Energy (AE/IE)

    So far, only the 'Relax Stiffness' control method has been tested.
    The houglass energy is only an issue for coarse mesh sizes (>0.05).
    It converges towards 0 with mesh size and mass scaling. For now, a mass scale of 500 is enough to get good results.

3. Total Energy Conservation (ETOTAL)

    The energy drift converges toward 0 with mesh size, reaching satifying values for size < 0.04 (0.036%).
    The reconstructed total energy is in perfect adequation with the abaqus one.

5. Force Magnitude

    The force increases with mesh size and is independent of mass scaling.
    The ALE tests have shown increases of about 10% for each decrease of size of 0.01.
    The no ALE and ALE tests seems to be converging to the same value, it is much faster without ALE, more results are needed to confirm this.

    Results have shown different order of magnitudes for elastomers (Conf 1) and vitreous polymers (Conf 2) going from a few mN to a few N.
    It is an expected result of the compressiblity of the material.

7. Friction Physics (SCOF)

    As expected, the SCOF is higher than mu, this should allow to estimate the effect of ploughing, which would be around 10% of mu.
    It converges towards an unknown value ( either 0.3 or 0.3xx ) with mesh size and is independent of mass scale.

8. Full Recovery

    The residual depth converges toward 0 with mesh size, thus helping to verify the validity of the model.

    An excessive mass scaling seems to decrease the recovery quality, which is also why 500 seems to be enough.
    This was only the case with ALE, no ALE simulations seems to give both better results and better mass scaling independency.

Annexes

Symbols and Units (SI mm system)

    C10, C01     Mooney-Rivlin material constants                    [MPa]
    D1           Compressibility parameter (= 2/K)                   [1/MPa]
    mu_0         Initial shear modulus = 2(C10 + C01)                [MPa]
    K_0          Initial bulk modulus = 2/D1                         [MPa]
    E_0          Initial Young's modulus = 9*K*mu/(3K+mu)            [MPa]
    nu_0         Initial Poisson's ratio = (3K-2mu)/(6K+2mu)         [-]
    rho          Density                                             [t/mm^3]
    R            Indenter tip radius                                 [mm]
    delta        Scratch (penetration) depth                         [mm]
    a            Contact radius = sqrt(delta*R)                      [mm]
    E*           Reduced modulus = E_0/(1-nu^2)                      [MPa]
    c_d          Dilatational wave speed = sqrt((K+4mu/3)/rho)       [mm/s]
    dt           Stable time increment = L_min / c_d                 [s]
    alpha        Mass scaling factor                                 [-]
    ALLKE        Total kinetic energy of the substrate               [mJ]
    ALLIE        Total internal (strain) energy of the substrate     [mJ]
    ALLAE        Total artificial (hourglass) energy                 [mJ]
    ETOTAL       Total energy of the whole model                     [mJ]
    ALLPW        Total contact penalty work                          [mJ]
    ALLFD        Total frictional dissipation                        [mJ]
    RF1          Reaction force, transverse (x) direction            [N]
    RF2          Reaction force, normal (y) direction                [N]
    RF3          Reaction force, scratch (z) direction               [N]
    SCOF         Apparent scratch coefficient of friction            [-]
    mu_a         Adhesive (interfacial) friction coefficient         [-]
    mu_p         Ploughing friction coefficient                      [-]
    eps_char     Tabor characteristic strain = 0.2*a/R               [-]
    eps_dot      Mean strain rate = eps_char / t_contact             [1/s]
